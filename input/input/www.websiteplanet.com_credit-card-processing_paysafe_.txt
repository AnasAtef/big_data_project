<h3>Decent Phone Support, but Other Support Channels Would Be Nice</h3>
<p><strong>Phone support for non-customers is only available during regular business hours,</strong> but it’s not clear exactly when this is. The after-hours phone message informed me that representatives are available from 9:00 a.m. to 7:30 p.m. (EST), but I found two instances on the website that quoted slightly different times.</p>
<p>However, once you’ve signed up, you’ll also be able to get support from your account manager through email and via phone support outside of business hours.</p>
<p>I called twice during business hours to ask a variety of questions I had about starting a new merchant account. <strong>Both times I was connected almost instantly with a customer service representative – and then promptly transferred to sales.</strong></p>
<p>Each time I called, <strong>the sales reps were incredibly patient and helpful, </strong>painstakingly answering all my questions<strong>,</strong> even as I constantly sought clarification. Neither were pushy about closing a sale and one encouraged me to shop around before committing.</p>
<p>At the end of one call, one sales representative, Nico, provided me with his direct line to make it easier to reach out with any other questions or to get started. <strong>I really appreciated the personalized approach he took without being too salesy. </strong></p>
<p>While the phone support was great, <strong>I found it frustrating that Paysafe didn’t have alternative means to contact support, especially as a non-customer</strong>. Whereas other processors offer live chat or even detailed online knowledge bases, Paysafe only has a single FAQ PDF that provides brief responses that ultimately aren’t very helpful.</p>
