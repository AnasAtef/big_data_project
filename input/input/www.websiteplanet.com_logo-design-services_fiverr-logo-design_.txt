<h3>What Exactly Does Fiverr Do and Is It A Contest-Based Service?</h3>
<figure class="wp-caption aligncenter"><span class="ll-img-wrapper "  style="padding:57.902973395931% 0 0; max-width: 850px;"><noscript><img src="https://dt2sdf0db8zob.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/image-2.png"  width="1278" height="740" alt="Fiverr Features" /></noscript><img data-src="https://dt2sdf0db8zob.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/image-2.png"   width="1278" height="740" alt="Fiverr Features" class="border ll-img"/></span><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Fiverr features gigs at many different price tags</figcaption></figure>
<p>This is normally where I write a joke, but Id like to get serious for half a moment. <strong>I dont like contest services much, as a designer myself.</strong> A lot of people put in a lot of work  some of it good, and some of it not so good  and only one of them gets paid.</p>
<p>When I review a contest-based design service, such as <strong><a href="https://www.websiteplanet.com/logo-design-services/99designs/" target="_blank" >99designs</a></strong>or <strong><a href="https://www.websiteplanet.com/logo-design-services/designcrowd/" target="_blank" >DesignCrowd</a></strong>, I do my best to ignore this and rate them on how well it performs the services it advertises. But to be honest, I dont like that business model at all.</p>
<p>Sure, its a very consumer-friendly way to run a business, but it can really screw over the designers.</p>
<p>Fiverr uses a model that I personally approve of: you browse around to find a designer you like, you look at their past work and what they offer for the prices they charge. <strong>You buy, they do design stuff, and everyone goes home with <em>something</em>. </strong>Just like with almost any other service, its up to the consumer to choose their designer wisely.</p>
<p>Oh, and the services offered are called Gigs. Just a good thing to know.</p>
<p>Fortunately, selecting a designer isnt too much of a hassle. Fiverr is a large community and <strong>theres a designer there for everybody</strong>. Some designers have a distinct style or can work with a variety of styles. There seem to be quite a few designers that specifically offer minimalist logos, but just about every style is represented somewhere within the website.</p>
<p><strong>Now, the number of options you get to choose from, the time it takes for the logos to be made, the number of revisions you get, and even the file formats available <em>all</em> depend (I told you) on the designer you choose and how much money you spend on them. </strong></p>
<p>For example, the cheaper plans often wont include vector or source files, while the more expensive plans might include all the file formats you want <em>and</em> stationery designs with your logo on them. Then again, some five-dollar (when I say dollar, I mean USD) designers <em>will</em> include vector files.</p>
<p>If you need to talk to your designers about revisions, or perhaps politely request that your logo files be sent in a less common format (like WEBP, or&#8230; CorelDraw?), <strong>you can contact them directly via Fiverrs messaging system.</strong> This system feels like a cross between a chat app and project management app feed.</p>
<p>You can, of course, rate designers, leave feedback and testimonials, see their past work, and more. In fact, this is highly encouraged, both by myself and the Fiverr platform.</p>
<h3><strong>You Can Hire Guaranteed Professionals</strong></h3>
<p><strong>This tends to be a more expensive option, but <em>theoretically</em>, its a safer option.</strong> Technically, anyone could upload a few designs they didnt make, call it their portfolio, and swindle people online. Now, these people usually receive ratings lower than Ben Afflecks <em>Daredevil</em> movie, but if you want to be very sure, you can search for Pros.</p>
<p>On Fiverr, Pros are creators and service providers that have been tested, vetted, and vouched for by Fiverr themselves. These people tend to be a bit more expensive than everyone else, but youre paying for a guarantee that even if things go wrong, Fiverr will remedy the situation (somehow).</p>
<h3><strong>Or You Can Hire Whole Studios</strong></h3>
<p><strong>If you want to hire a whole team of people, but dont want to spend all of your time coordinating them, you can just hire a whole studio to work on your project. </strong>Get your branding direction, logo design, and website done all at the same time. Maybe throw in some copywriting for good measure.</p>
<h3><strong>Fiverr Has an Active Community to Help You out</strong></h3>
<p>It has events for creators, a blog, and even a podcast if thats your thing. As a customer, you may be more interested in the forum, where you can get some community support regarding the website itself, the Fiverr apps, and even get opinions on designs that other people are making for you.</p>
<h3>You Can Hire People for Almost Anything on Fiverr</h3>
<p><strong>Fiverr is <em>not</em> technically a design website.</strong> Its a website where you can hire people to make videos, edit videos, write things, make music, make presentations, provide marketing services, do research, or even perform <a href="https://www.websiteplanet.com/blog/weirdest-gigs-on-fiverr/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>really weird jobs</strong></a>. The possibilities are endless.</p>
<p>In a way, Fiverr is closer to Craigs List than it is to, for example, 99designs. The difference is that there arent any ads about missed connections, but you could hire someone to <em>make</em> you a Craigs List ad about missed connections.</p>

<p>Im going to take a moment to let you in on a little secret: <strong>not only am I buying logos from all of these different services to review them, but were going to pick the best logo to use on Website Planet.</strong> In other words: FIGHT. FIGHT. FIGH *ahem*, I mean may the best designer win.</p>
<p>My journey with Fiverr was a long one, compared to that of most reviews, because I was working with three different designers. I hired one whose prices start at $5, and went up from there, with our most expensive logo coming in at several hundred dollars.</p>

Slow loading times is the only reason Fiverr is losing any points in this section.</p>

<h3>How Easy Is It to Use Fiverr?</h3>
<figure class="wp-caption aligncenter"><span class="ll-img-wrapper "  style="padding:52.098540145985% 0 0; max-width: 850px;"><noscript><img src="https://dt2sdf0db8zob.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/image-1.png"  width="1096" height="571" alt="Fiverr profile - screenshot" /></noscript><img data-src="https://dt2sdf0db8zob.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/image-1.png"   width="1096" height="571" alt="Fiverr profile - screenshot" class="border ll-img"/></span><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Always contact your seller to communicate your ideas before placing an order</figcaption></figure>
<p>The website and service as designed by the Fiverr team are pretty good, and fairly easy to use. Not everything is self-explanatory, and you might need to refer to the wiki/knowledge base now and then if youve never used a website like it before, but its not hard to figure out.</p>
<p><strong>However, individual profiles and service offerings (the Gigs), are written by the people offering their services, and not (in most cases) by copywriters. </strong>They tend to use industry jargon and dont always write in full sentences.</p>
<p>Although not always a clear indicator of skill and ability, a polished profile shows that the freelancer cares.</p>
<p>No, but seriously. The more highly-rated Gigs and profiles are usually easier to understand. Thats probably where you already planned to start your search, but now you have an extra reason to carry on as you were.</p>
<h3><strong>You Can Filter People/Gigs Easily</strong></h3>
<p>The Gig finder/browser is pretty easy to use. For logo designers, <strong>you can browse freelancers by style, price range, delivery time, profile rating, and more.</strong></p>
<h3><strong>You Can Save Gigs to Look at Later</strong></h3>
<p>Its not the same as saving a project or contest you havent finished yet. Its more like collecting all of your candidates in one place, so you can make your final decision later. You can favorite the Gigs you like, sort them into lists, and generally keep things organized.</p>
<h3><strong>Fiverr Will Guide You Through the Process of Detailing Your Requirements</strong></h3>
<p>Once you decide on a Gig you like, Fiverr will ask you a series of questions to help you detail your requirements. These questions depend on what service youre actually getting, of course. <strong>For logos, it asks for things like the name you want to incorporate, the tagline, any color preferences you might have, extra notes.</strong> You know, all the standard logo stuff.</p>
<p>Overall, Fiverr is not too confusing and it costs nothing to look. You dont even have to sign up for an account to browse around the website and check out designers/other service providers. Designer profiles include ratings, testimonials, examples of their past work, a full list of all the services they offer, and even which languages they speak.</p>
<p><strong>Actually working with the designers is simple: all of your messages and files for a particular Gig are kept in the same place.</strong> Aside from the aforementioned issues with actually loading the website, I have no real complaints.</p>

<h3>What Support Options Does Fiverr Offer?</h3>
<p><strong>Fiverrs support is the kind you get from a single parent who is loving and caring but has six other kids, and a lot of work to do.</strong> Theyll get to you, and theyll provide good support, but it might take a minute.</p>
<p> Was that too personal? Whatever, its a perfect analogy, so its staying in unless my editor decides otherwise.</p>
<p>Help (from Fiverr, not my hard-working mother) is provided in one of three ways:<strong> theres a knowledge base, a ticket system, and a community forum. <strong>The knowledge base is extensive</strong></strong>, though it didnt answer <em>all</em> of my personal questions. Even so, it is well organized and keeps the buyer-help articles and seller-help articles nicely separate. Id highly encourage you to look there first if youre having a problem.</p>
<p>As for the human support:</p>
<h3><strong>Ticket Support</strong></h3>
<p><strong>The ticket system is meant to be used for specific issues, which you can select through a series of drop-down menus.</strong> If your kind of problem isnt on the list, there really isnt a general category you could use. General or oddball problems seem to be what the forum is for.</p>
<p>The estimated response time is 24-48 hours, though both of my tickets were resolved in less than a day. It does say that you shouldnt open multiple tickets at a time if you can help it, as that slows response times down (not exactly sure how, but&#8230; [shrugs]). Please note that while you can accidentally create duplicate tickets (it happened to me), you wont be penalized for that.</p>
<figure id="attachment_897643" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-897643"class="wp-caption aligncenter"><span class="ll-img-wrapper "  style="padding:42.406876790831% 0 0; max-width: 850px;"><noscript><img src="https://dt2sdf0db8zob.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/fiverr-logo-design-support2-e1683809616313.jpg"  width="1047" height="444" alt="Fiverr Help &amp; Education Center - screenshot" /></noscript><img data-src="https://dt2sdf0db8zob.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/fiverr-logo-design-support2-e1683809616313.jpg"   width="1047" height="444" alt="Fiverr Help &amp; Education Center - screenshot" class="border wp-image-897643 size-full ll-img"/></span><figcaption id="caption-attachment-897643"class="wp-caption-text">Dont mind the duplicate ticket!</figcaption></figure>

<p><strong>Depending on your question, the community forum can be a hit or miss experience.</strong> I see plenty of people asking questions, and at least getting some sort of response in a few hours. Me, on the other hand</p>
<p>I asked if there was some sort of a light version of the website (no, the mobile app doesnt count), and at the time of this writing, I havent gotten any sort of response in two days. Not even a no.</p>

<p>Let&#8217;s unveil the pricing details with a drum roll, please!
