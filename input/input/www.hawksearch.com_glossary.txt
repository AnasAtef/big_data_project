class="feature-content-rich-text w-dyn-bind-empty w-richtext"></div></div></div><div role="listitem" class="glossary-collection-item w-dyn-item"><div class="glossary-letter-group w-condition-invisible"><h2 class="glossary-letter-group-heading">AI (Artificial Intelligence)</h2></div><div class="glossary-card"><div class="glossary-title-wrap"><h3 class="list-title-h3 glossary">AI (Artificial Intelligence)</h3></div><a href="#" class="glossary-link-wrap w-inline-block w-condition-invisible"><h3 class="list-title-h3">AI (Artificial Intelligence)</h3><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/616ef101d9f2a3350a5daaf5/653a74b51889f4f91ec4d2d4_external-link.svg" loading="lazy" alt="external link icon" class="image-77"/></a><div class="feature-content-rich-text w-richtext"><p>Artificial Intelligence, or AI, is a cutting-edge field in computer science that&#x27;s all about creating technology capable of doing things we usually think only humans can do. This includes everything from figuring out patterns in data and making decisions, to solving problems and learning from experience. AI is a big deal across a bunch of different areas, especially in online shopping where it helps make search results better, in predicting future trends, handling customer service through chatbots, and even in tailoring marketing to fit each person&#x27;s preferences. Basically, AI is streamlining and smartening up a whole range of tasks, making decisions smarter and customer service more personalized, all thanks to the power of automation and smart data use.</p></div></div></div><div role="listitem" class="glossary-collection-item w-dyn-item"><div class="glossary-letter-group w-condition-invisible"><h2 class="glossary-letter-group-heading">AI Ethics</h2></div><div class="glossary-card"><div class="glossary-title-wrap"><h3 class="list-title-h3 glossary">AI Ethics</h3></div><a href="#" class="glossary-link-wrap w-inline-block w-condition-invisible"><h3 class="list-title-h3">AI Ethics</h3><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/616ef101d9f2a3350a5daaf5/653a74b51889f4f91ec4d2d4_external-link.svg" loading="lazy" alt="external link icon" class="image-77"/></a><div class="feature-content-rich-text w-richtext"><p>AI Ethics encompasses the moral principles and practices guiding the development, deployment, and use of artificial intelligence technologies. It addresses issues like bias, privacy, autonomy, and impact on employment. Including &quot;ethical AI development,&quot; &quot;AI impact on society,&quot; and &quot;responsible AI&quot; in the description can engage readers interested in the societal implications of AI.</p></div></div></div><div role="listitem" class="glossary-collection-item w-dyn-item"><div class="glossary-letter-group w-condition-invisible"><h2 class="glossary-letter-group-heading">Activation Function </h2></div><div class="glossary-card"><div class="glossary-title-wrap"><h3 class="list-title-h3 glossary">Activation Function </h3></div><a href="#" class="glossary-link-wrap w-inline-block w-condition-invisible"><h3 class="list-title-h3">Activation Function </h3><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/616ef101d9f2a3350a5daaf5/653a74b51889f4f91ec4d2d4_external-link.svg" loading="lazy" alt="external link icon" class="image-77"/></a><div class="feature-content-rich-text w-richtext"><p>Activation functions in neural networks are like the decision-makers that determine whether a neuron should spring into action or not, depending on the importance of the information it receives. They&#x27;re the secret sauce that allows these networks to deal with complicated, real-world data by adding a dash of non-linearity, meaning they can handle the twists and turns of unpredictable information. These functions are super important for creating deep learning models that do all sorts of clever things, from recognizing faces in photos to understanding human language, and even making online shopping searches smarter. Essentially, they help models make sense of complex data, spot patterns, and learn from what they&#x27;re fed, enabling all kinds of smart technology we use every day.</p></div></div></div><div role="listitem" class="glossary-collection-item w-dyn-item"><div class="glossary-letter-group w-condition-invisible"><h2 class="glossary-letter-group-heading">Attention Mechanism</h2></div><div class="glossary-card"><div class="glossary-title-wrap"><h3 class="list-title-h3 glossary">Attention Mechanism</h3></div><a href="#" class="glossary-link-wrap w-inline-block w-condition-invisible"><h3 class="list-title-h3">Attention Mechanism</h3><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/616ef101d9f2a3350a5daaf5/653a74b51889f4f91ec4d2d4_external-link.svg" loading="lazy" alt="external link icon" class="image-77"/></a><div class="feature-content-rich-text w-richtext"><p>The attention mechanism in deep learning is a clever trick used in models, especially for processing and understanding human language, that lets them zero in on the most important parts of the data they&#x27;re working with. Imagine it like having the ability to focus intently on the most crucial bits of a conversation or a book to understand the meaning better. This not only makes these models really good at tasks like translating languages, summarizing articles, or answering questions but also powers up other AI areas. For example, it helps computers recognize images more accurately and even makes online shopping recommendations more personal and on-point. Essentially, this mechanism helps models sift through and prioritize vast amounts of information, making technologies smarter and more useful for us.</p></div></div></div><div role="listitem" class="glossary-collection-item w-dyn-item"><div class="glossary-letter-group"><h2 id="B" class="glossary-letter-group-heading">B</h2></div><div class="glossary-card w-condition-invisible"><div class="glossary-title-wrap"><h3 class="list-title-h3 glossary">B</h3></div><a href="#" class="glossary-link-wrap w-inline-block w-condition-invisible"><h3 class="list-title-h3">B</h3><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/616ef101d9f2a3350a5daaf5/653a74b51889f4f91ec4d2d4_external-link.svg" loading="lazy" alt="external link icon" class="image-77"/></a><div class="feature-content-rich-text w-dyn-bind-empty w-richtext"></div></div></div><div role="listitem" class="glossary-collection-item w-dyn-item"><div class="glossary-letter-group w-condition-invisible"><h2 class="glossary-letter-group-heading">Backpropagation</h2></div><div class="glossary-card"><div class="glossary-title-wrap"><h3 class="list-title-h3 glossary">Backpropagation</h3></div><a href="#" class="glossary-link-wrap w-inline-block w-condition-invisible"><h3 class="list-title-h3">Backpropagation</h3><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/616ef101d9f2a3350a5daaf5/653a74b51889f4f91ec4d2d4_external-link.svg" loading="lazy" alt="external link icon" class="image-77"/></a><div class="feature-content-rich-text w-richtext"><p>Backpropagation is a method used to train neural networks, where the network learns from examples. Imagine it as a way of fine-tuning the network&#x27;s guesses. It works by looking at the mistakes the network makes (via a loss function) and then going backwards, adjusting all the weights inside the network little by little. This adjustment is done using something called the chain rule, which is a way to calculate these changes efficiently. In simpler terms, backpropagation helps the network learn from its errors, making its future guesses more accurate.</p></div></div></div><div role="listitem" class="glossary-collection-item w-dyn-item"><div class="glossary-letter-group w-condition-invisible"><h2 class="glossary-letter-group-heading">Bayesian Networks</h2></div><div class="glossary-card"><div class="glossary-title-wrap"><h3 class="list-title-h3 glossary">Bayesian Networks</h3></div><a href="#" class="glossary-link-wrap w-inline-block w-condition-invisible"><h3 class="list-title-h3">Bayesian Networks</h3><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/616ef101d9f2a3350a5daaf5/653a74b51889f4f91ec4d2d4_external-link.svg" loading="lazy" alt="external link icon" class="image-77"/></a><div class="feature-content-rich-text w-richtext"><p>Bayesian Networks are a way to model how different pieces of information or variables relate to each other, especially when things aren&#x27;t 100% certain. Imagine a flowchart where each point is a piece of information that can affect or be affected by others, but instead of definite outcomes, there are probabilities or chances. These networks map out all the possible relationships and their conditions using a specific kind of diagram known as a directed acyclic graph (DAG), which means the connections between points don&#x27;t loop back on themselves.
